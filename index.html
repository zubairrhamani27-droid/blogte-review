What is Blogte.com (Trace Lost Crypto)?

Blogte.com presents itself as a crypto and fund recovery blog under the brand name "Trace Lost Crypto." The site positions itself as a consumer advocate platform that publishes reviews of broker scams and claims it can help victims recover their lost cryptocurrency or trading funds.

At first glance, this may seem like a legitimate service. However, after careful analysis, the platform displays textbook warning signs of what is known in the industry as a "Recovery Scam" — a secondary fraud that specifically targets people who have already been victimized once and are desperate to get their money back.

🚩 Major Red Flags

  • 💰
    Upfront Fee Demands Recovery scam operators ask for an advance fee, "investigation deposit," or "processing charge" before they begin any "recovery work." Once paid, they disappear or ask for more money.
  • 🕵️
    Complete Anonymity of Operators There is no verifiable information about who owns or operates Blogte.com. No real names, no company registration numbers, no physical office addresses are provided.
  • ❌
    No Regulatory Authorization Legitimate fund recovery services (solicitors, licensed financial investigators) are registered with law enforcement or financial regulatory bodies. Blogte.com holds no such credentials.
  • 🔁
    Targets Previously Scammed Victims The site publishes reviews of known scam brokers to rank in search engines for terms like "I was scammed by [broker]" — specifically to attract already-victimized, vulnerable users.
  • ⚙️
    Impossible Recovery Promises Blockchain transactions are irreversible by design. Any entity claiming they can definitively recover crypto sends to a scam wallet is making a technically false promise. No tool, software, or "hacker" can reverse confirmed blockchain transactions.

🔶 The "Recovery Scam" Pattern Explained

The scheme is simple and effective: a victim loses $10,000 to a fake broker. They search online for help, find a "recovery service" like Blogte.com. The service asks for $500–$2,000 upfront to "begin the investigation." After the fee is paid — either more fees are requested, or contact simply stops. The victim is now scammed twice. Do not engage with any unsolicited or unverified recovery service online.

What Should You Actually Do If You Were Scammed?

If you have lost money to a fraudulent broker or crypto scheme, these are the only legitimate steps you should take:

  • Report the fraud to your national financial regulator (e.g., FCA in the UK, CFTC/SEC in the USA).
  • If you paid by credit card, contact your bank immediately and request a chargeback under Section 75.
  • File a police report with your local law enforcement's cybercrime unit.
  • Report to the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) at ic3.gov.
  • Never pay any upfront fees to a "recovery" service — this is always another scam.

🔴 Final Verdict: AVOID

Blogte.com (Trace Lost Crypto) is highly likely to be a recovery scam operation. It preys on vulnerable crypto fraud victims by promising to recover lost funds in exchange for advance fees. There is no legitimate basis for their claims and no regulatory authorization behind their "services." If you have been contacted by this site, do not send any money and report it to your local consumer protection authority immediately.
